# Email Threat Types

- **Phishing**: Mass emails impersonating trusted brands to harvest credentials
  or payment details through fake login pages.
- **Spear phishing**: Highly targeted phishing that references real names,
  projects, or colleagues to appear legitimate.
- **Business Email Compromise (BEC)**: Attacker impersonates an executive or
  vendor to request wire transfers or gift-card purchases; often has no links.
- **Malware / ransomware delivery**: Malicious attachments or links that install
  software to steal data or encrypt files for ransom.
- **Scam / fraud**: Lottery wins, inheritance offers, or "you won a prize"
  messages designed to extract fees or personal data.
- **Spam**: Unsolicited bulk mail; usually low risk but can carry the threats
  above.

Risk should be scored higher when the message combines impersonation, urgency,
and a call to action such as clicking a link or sending money.
